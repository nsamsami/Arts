Bridging the Arts at University California of Berkeley is a program that engages one K-12 students, UCB students and teachers in Oakland Unified and West Contra Costa Unified School Districts. The schools we currently serve include Coronado Elementary, Hoover Elementary and Helms Middle School.

Many public schools in the Bay Area have little or no arts programming or curricula that specifically address the California State Standards. Bridging the Arts seeks to expand the opportunities and address the social inequalities in arts education by facilitating access to lessons/lesson plans, promoting education successes/retention, and college-going interests.

Bridging the Arts provides its students with benefits directly related to arts learning but also to writing, vocabulary, participation, and collaboration. Bridging the Arts projects demonstrate that students who have worked with art processes actively use cognitive skills and problem solving that can be useful in many other subjects.

UCB Bridging the Arts Student Instructors are a significant influence and may be the only exposure K-12 students will get to college students. Of note are the transformative abilities of the collaborations to engage, excite, and promote learning. Bridging the Arts is the only program on campus that presents K-12 students and cooperative teachers with the opportunity to integrate art into their pre-existing curricula, providing schools with UCB student instructors, role models and accessible practicing artists.
